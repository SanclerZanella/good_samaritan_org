Who We Are

Poor children in Mozambique only eat once a day, usually at 6 pm. Among the causes of hunger are drought, not appropriate cultivation land, undiversified type of cultivation, corruption in politics and others. The National Institute of Statistics in Mozambique indicates that 35% of the population live in chronic food insecurity and 43% of children 0-5 years suffer from chronic malnutrition.

Many children have inadequate nutrient intake due to a poor diet, infectious diseases, especially malaria, diarrheal diseases and AIDS, in short, poverty. about one and a half million people live with the AIDS virus in Mozambique, and only 640,000 seek treatment. About a third of the infected population abandons treatment in the first year, due to lack of food and information, but also due to lack of resources in the national health system.

In an interview with a widow named Sara, she said that she lost her husband in 2016 to AIDS. Also infected, she says that she has abandoned the treatment because the treatment is too strong and she needs to eat something before taking the medicine. Their children do not eat during the day and are always without strength to play with their friends.

How does the Good Samaritan organization arise in Mozambique?

The care for the community of Quelimane in Mozambique began with Pastor Danilo Montanha and his family concerned about the precarious situation of orphaned children, widows and abandoned elderly in their community, living with extreme poverty.

After being dismissed from Concern Worldwide, the company he worked for, he received compensation of 150,000 meticais, equivalent to $2,350.70. For some years, the community was supported by Danilo and his wife, with their local efforts.

In 2015, through the internet, pastor Danilo met the Brazilian pastor Leonidas Duarte, who embraced the project and has worked tirelessly in search of help for this place. Today we care for 80 orphaned and vulnerable children, 48 widows and 15 elderly people. We take care of the distribution of food, clothing, school supplies and agricultural resources.

In July 2017, the village was visited by the baker Alex Ribeiro Duarte, who taught the community how to make bread from natural sources and created a structure with ovens and other necessary equipment so that the community could produce bread and thus survive by producing its own food.

Main Changes In The Village

  • Access to clothing in good condition;
  • Access to more than one meal a day;
  • Training for making bread;
  • Training to sew clothes;
  • Access to school supplies;
  • Hope for a better future.

Challenges

  • Lack of enough food for the daily routine;
  • Lack of an appropriate building that works as an orphanage center to house our children;
  • Lack of means of transport that would facilitate the transport of children, widows and the elderly, on the way to medical appointments and school;
  • Lack of a local chicken breeding project to produce eggs and meat to feed children;

Perspectives

  • To be able to provide assistance to orphans and vulnerable children, establishing a shelter that works as an orphanage center and also in the construction of a teaching site for pre-school learning, as the government of Mozambique does not provide pre-school education;
  • Develop agricultural programs for the development of the village, through self-sufficiency, obtained from the population in the production of seeds, vegetables, as well as the raising of chicken;
