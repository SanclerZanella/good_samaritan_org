Facts About Mozambique

  • Nearly a million people are hungry in Mozambique, UN warns.

    - Publico

  • Orphans are also among the most vulnerable in Mozambique, where the number of orphans who have lost one or both parents is estimated to be 2 million. Another 700,000 children are at risk of being abandoned because their caregivers are elderly, HIV in the family and/or deteriorating socio-economic circumstances.

    - Unicef

  • The widowhood leaves women even more vulnerable, leading them to instability that has various implications, such as their social exclusion and that of their children, the breakdown and disintegration of the family fabric, social discrimination, among other factors.

    - wlsa.org

  • Rejected by their own family, some elderly people are forced to live on the streets or in shelters. A situation that has been getting worse. In 2016 alone, the police registered hundreds of cases of abandoned elderly people.

    - Deutsche Welle

  • Since 2016, The Good Samaritan has been feeding orphan children, providing a home and education to change their reality and give a good future for these children.

  • Welcoming widows in the project, giving them a profession, food and a dignified way to live in a society that generally despises them.

  • And taking care of elderly, giving them medical care, food and home.
